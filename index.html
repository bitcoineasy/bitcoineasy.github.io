What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is digital cash that can be used without a third party (a company between the sender and the receiver).

Why is this significant? It is very fast, works 24/7 and you can send it anywhere in the world to anybody you want. No bank account, credit card, or paypal account required.

Bitcoin is the most secure computer network in the world. Since its invention in 2009 it has never been hacked. The "Bitcoin hacks" that you have heard about involved companies that didn't protect the private keys well enough. Imagine it like this: Bitcoin is like Gold in an indestructible safe. You can only get it if you find the key.

How is Bitcoin different from Paypal, Apple Pay or other payment providers?

Banks and payment providers are centralized. This means there is one server with all the information on who has how much and where the money is sent. Since they control these servers, they can decide who is allowed to send and receive payments and who isn't. Bitcoin is decentralized. Everybody can have a copy of the "bitcoin server" (Bitcoin full-node) with all the transactions ever made.

There is no "Bitcoin company" and you don't need to sign up anywhere. All you need is a Bitcoin wallet. There are paper wallets, software wallets and hardware wallets. These wallets contain the keys to the Bitcoins.

Bitcoins are not stored on your computer or phone, they are stored on the decentralized Bitcoin computer network (Blockchain) and can be accessed with a password (also called private key).

Bitcoin is permissionless. This means that you can use Bitcoin to accept payments for your business without signing up anywhere or doing any paperwork. Just post your Bitcoin address or qr code on your website.

Does Bitcoin unnecessarily waste too much energy?

Many pepole think that Bitcoin unnecessarily wastes energy because of an arbitrary algorithm that could simply be changed to a different energy saving algorithm. Bitcoin mining is not an algorithm and it is not arbitrary. Mining is what makes Bitcoin the most secure computer network in the world. It takes energy, an expensive resource from the real world, and uses it in a security mechanism in the digital world to solve the double-spending problem.

To attack Bitcoin you need to spend 51% of the entire energy of the Bitcoin network for a prolonged period of time. As I explain in "Could a state attack Bitcoin?" this is very difficult to do.

The double-spending problem shortly explained: In the digital world, you can send the same file or email as often as you want. A digital money system only works if this is not possible. Every unit of digital money can only be sent once. the great innovation in Bitcoin is that it solved this problem of digital scarcity.

The world's hydro, biofuel, solar and wind energy combined could power the Bitcoin network 98 times. The amount of electricity consumed every year in always-on but inactive home devices in the USA alone could power the Bitcoin network for 3.5 years. See these comparisons and others here: cbeci.org/comparisons

As you can see, there is no reason to be afraid of Bitcoins energy consumption.

Is Bitcoin better than normal money?

There will only ever be 21 million Bitcoin. "Normal money" like US Dollars or Euros dont have a fixed supply and can be created by the government and by private banks for their personal advantage. This gives them too much power and inflates the money supply. The rising money supply causes the money to loose value every year. Bitcoin on the other hand can not be inflated by governments or private banks. In Bitcoin, the same rules apply for everybody.

How does Bitcoin work?

Bitcoins are created in a process called mining which also makes Bitcoin so secure. Computers do a lot of calculations and get Bitcoins as a reward. To "hack" Bitcoin you would need at least 51% of all the energy that all Bitcoin miners in the entire world are using for a prolonged period of time.

Could a state attack Bitcoin?

A State can not buy enough hardware for a 51% attack. The number of manufacturers is well known and there is a shortage of miners. All manufacturing facilities are running at full capacity producing chips for graphics cards, AI and supercomputers. Because the existing installed number of miners is 4 or 5 times more than the annual production, a nation state would need to buy for 2 years, all the supply of hardware manufacturers worldwide, just to be able to reach 51%. This makes entering the market secretly impossible.

Only theoretically can a state manufacture enough miners for a 51% attack. It has to spend billions for the facilities, billions for the production and hundreds of millions for the energy and would not achieve much. With a 51% attack you do not control Bitcoin. The only thing you can do is make fake transactions and block transactions for a short period of time. Both attacks are identifiable and can be remedied by the Bitcoin ecosystem.

The state can get mining rewards about half the time but these Bitcoins would be identifiable and can be rejected by the Bitcoin community. Hence, the state can not profit from mining and would be competing with profitable miners.

After a short period of time, the Bitcoin ecosystem would decide to fork off the attacking state miners and cause a chain split (bifurcation of the bitcoin network into two):

1. original-bitcoin-chain. 2. state-attack-chain.

After this bifurcation the attacking state miners will be mining on the state-attack-chain and not affect the original-bitcoin-chain anymore.

The result of this failed nation state attack would increase the confidence in Bitcoin and send Bitcoin prices soaring.
